About Us

We are a group of individuals who are committed to stopping deforestation in Indonesia. Our mission is to raise awareness and gather support to put an end to this destructive practice.

Our Mission

Our mission is to save the Indonesian rainforest from deforestation. We believe that by spreading awareness and encouraging people to take action, we can make a difference.

We want to create a world where future generations can enjoy the beauty and benefits of the rainforest. Join us in our mission to stop deforestation in Indonesia.

Our Efforts

We work to raise awareness about the impact of deforestation in Indonesia and to promote sustainable forest management practices. We also support reforestation efforts and work to protect existing forests.

Deforestation

Due to continual population growth, forests are often cut down to make space for farms and pastures. By cutting forests down, carbon emissions are released into the environment, further contributing to global warming. While this initial release of carbon is slightly harmful to the environment, deforestation presents major issues in the loss of a carbon sink, soil erosion, and loss of biodiversity. From 1990-2020, Indonesia had a large change in forest area and even lost over 100,000 square miles. This reflects major deforestation in Indonesia and manifests the growing concern of climate change by reducing the world’s natural carbon sink, losing and eroding soil, and contributing to a loss in biodiversity.

Possible Solutions

Promoting Sustainability and Regrowth of Forests

A possible solution towards solving deforestation in Indonesia is by promoting the sustainability and regrowth of forests. Since Indonesia has a major lumber industry, with it’s value reaching over 12 billion USD, completely stopping deforestation would be inconceivable. However, the current rate of deforestation in Indonesia presents worries for growing climate change. Thus, organizations like Team Trees or movements that promote sustainability education and work to establish tree nurseries or the planting of new trees. This solution would influence communities through means like social media and would take root in social groups throughout Indonesia by inspiring individuals to take part in restoring the forestry in Indonesia.

Foreign Investment in Export Area Shift

As deforestation is a global problem, one solution would be to allow for foreign entities (from both private and public/governmental) to easily invest in an export area shift within Indonesia. Governments around the world can also support low interest loans for Indonesia, which would help speed up the shift in Indonesia’s export focus from deforestation to other technologies. Indonesia is also a country with one of the highest technological advancements in the 21st century, with a sizable population to sustain the growth, so it will be of the investor’s interest to invest in Indonesian growth away from deforestation.
